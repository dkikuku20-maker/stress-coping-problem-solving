#  Exploring the Link between Stress, Coping Strategies, and Problem-Solving Skills in Higher Education

**Author:** Dessailly Kikuku  
**Institution:** University of Alberta  

---

##  Overview
This research investigates how stress, coping strategies, and problem-solving skills interact among college students. The goal was to identify which coping mechanisms are most effective in managing stress and enhancing problem-solving ability.

---

##  Key Findings
- **Negative correlation** between stress and problem-solving skills: students with stronger problem-solving ability experience lower stress.  
- **Problem-focused coping** strategies are more effective than emotion-focused ones.  
- Students who rely on problem-focused coping demonstrate higher problem-solving capability and lower perceived stress.  

---

##  Implications
Encouraging students to develop **problem-focused coping skills** can help reduce stress and improve academic performance.  
The study supports implementing **workshops and interventions** in higher education focused on building cognitive resilience and effective problem-solving.

---

##  Research Context
This study contributes to both:
- **Cognitive Psychology:** by linking stress management to cognitive processes and problem-solving.
- **Developmental Psychology:** emphasizing how coping and problem-solving affect growth and adaptation during college years.
